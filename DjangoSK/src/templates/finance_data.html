WHAT THE DATA REPRESENTS

Text mining tasks, such as document clustering and topic modeling, have been widely used for analyzing scientific publications. Understanding the trend of topics in scientific literature is of unique interest; however, time is often neglected as a feature in these text-mining algorithms. In particular, it is important to choose an appropriate performance metric to evaluate the result of these unsupervised clustering and topic modeling, and many available metrics do not accommodate a desire for temporally-clustered results that are typical of trends.

For this approach, a weighted temporal feature to bias a topic clustering toward articles in a similar time frame is introduced; this is performed over a set of finance journal abstracts from 1974 to 2020. Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is used to parameterize each abstract, followed by dimensionality reduction using Singular Value Decomposition (SVD). Trending topics in the field of finance are detected, topics that are not identifiable when using the standard clustering algorithm with no temporal bias.

To identify trending topics, a metric of the silhouette score divided by the standard deviation of cluster over time is utilized. Then isolate topics identified by this metric and evaluate them using expert judgment. This clustering strategy using temporal bias is also readily amenable to analysis in other fields for discovering the rise and fall of trends.
